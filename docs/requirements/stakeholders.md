# Stakeholders

Status: in progress

## Sponsor, owner, decision-maker

- Philip is sponsor, owner, sole developer, and sole decision-maker/reviewer for requirements and sign-off (a senior leader within the programme with authority to approve all decisions). No other party has a formal say in requirements or sign-off.
- This is distinct from the **system's user base**, which is multi-role: teachers, parents, and students all need direct access to the system (this is not a single-user tool). "Solo" describes delivery/ownership only.

## User personas

Status: in progress — carried over from the prior build as a starting hypothesis, being confirmed for this rebuild:
- Admin/Leader (Philip)
- Teacher
- Approver — **curriculum/lesson-change approval only**; no role in consent or DBS approval, and no access to DBS records
- Parent/Guardian
- Student
- Safeguarding Officer — safeguarding/consent/DBS oversight; alongside Admin, one of only two roles with access to DBS records

**Scale:** small — tens of users per role (not hundreds), at launch and expected over ~2 years.

**Role structure:** Approver and Safeguarding Officer are already held by named individuals distinct from Philip (Admin) — these are genuinely separate roles in practice today, not just future-delegation placeholders.
